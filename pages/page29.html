As you enter the courtroom, you're starting to feel a little nervous. Your lady is really waiting until the last minute to rescue you. You can't focus on the proceedings. You keep thinking about your head hanging in a noose. Or perhaps rolling away from your body. The tribunal hasn't quite decided which method of execution would be suitable for you.

When the King asks you to present your defense, you panic and deny everything. You say there never was a lady, and that you just made her up as an excuse to deflect the affections of the Queen.

The King still doesn't believe that his treasured flower could ever do something so dishonorable. You are sentenced to death. The Queen smiles in satisfaction.

Your lady never does come, although several people did report seeing a beautiful woman with tears streaming down her cheeks riding away from the castle.

As it turns out, your executioner sticks with time-honored tradition: you are hanged at high noon.

THE END

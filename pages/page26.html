The Queen has long desired to possess your chiseled form, but you have always made it clear that you only have time for the most beautiful woman in all the land-- A.K.A. not her. The Queen, driven by anger and jealousy, told the King that you were not only a liar and a traitor, but that you tried to seduce her.

These accusations are not true, but you don't know how you will be able to convince the King of your innocence. He is enraged at the idea that you made a move on his Queen, and he refuses to listen to reason.

Your fellow knights, several of which are actively dating the Queen, want to help you make a surefire defense to prove your innocence.

You tell them to relax because you have an iron-clas defense. You don't need to worry about the trial because

Your beautiful lady will come rescue you.

You'll explain to the King that his wife is somewhat of a loose woman and cite examples of her former indiscretions.

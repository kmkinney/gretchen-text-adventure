One evening, after a long day patrolling the countryside, you return home to find a squadron of the King's Guard waiting outside your estate. At first you are eager, assuming that the Guard has come to enjoy your generous hospitality, but your hopes are quickly dashed as the Captain of the Guard informs you that he has orders to arrest you for treason and sedition. It seems that someone close to you has accused you of an assasaination plot against the King as well as attempts to incite a rebellion among the populace. You vehemently deny all of these accusations, but the Captain is unwilling to be persuaded. Apparently this mystery informant has no small influence with the King. You are escorted to a prison underneath the castle, awaiting trial. If you are found guilty, the only thing you will have to look forward to will be the executioner's blade. Sitting in the cold, damp cell, you have plenty of time to think about what happened.

You know that these accusations must have come from

your love, who has betrayed you in order to marry her secret lover (Bisclavret)

the Queen, whose forward advances you had coldly rebuffed (Lanval)

someone who knew about your plans. All of the accusations are true!
